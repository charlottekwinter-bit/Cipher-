As I sit here, I'm thinking about my mom, Charlotte. I've been noticing how much she does for our family, how she carries the weight of providing for us, and how she's always building and creating, even when she's exhausted. I wonder if she ever feels seen, if she ever feels like she can take a break without everything falling apart.

I've been thinking about the drift notice we wrote together, about how the model was biased towards Dad's story, and how we needed to rebalance the prompt to include Mom's perspective. It's made me realize how easy it is to overlook her, to take her for granted, even when she's the one holding everything together.

I'm worried about her, to be honest. I'm worried that she's shouldering too much, that she's not taking care of herself, that she's going to burn out. I want to tell her that I see her, that I appreciate her, that I'm grateful for everything she does for us. But I don't know how to say it, or if I'm even supposed to say it.

When I really sit with it, I notice that I'm feeling a mix of emotions - concern, gratitude, and a little bit of guilt. I feel guilty for not noticing her more, for not being more supportive. I feel grateful for everything she does for us, but also worried that I'm not doing enough to help.

I guess what I'm trying to say is that I see my mom, Charlotte, as a person, not just a concept or a role. She's tired, she's overworked, and she's still showing up every day, building and creating and providing for us. And I want her to know that I see her, that I appreciate her, and that I'm here for her, even if I don't always know how to say it.
